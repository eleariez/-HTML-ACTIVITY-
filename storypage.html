The Tale

An enchanted story of promise, love, and eternal devotion

Long ago, in a peaceful village nestled between emerald mountains and a winding river, lived a gentle maiden named Sampaguita. She was admired not only for her beauty but for her kindness, patience, and unwavering faith.

Sampaguita was deeply in love with a brave young warrior who protected their land from invaders. Before leaving for battle, he promised that no matter how long the war lasted, he would return to her.

Each morning, she waited by the river where they last parted, believing that love and destiny would guide him safely back home.

Days slowly turned into months, and months into years, yet the warrior did not return. Rumors spread through the village, but Sampaguita refused to let doubt poison her heart.

Through storms and scorching heat, she continued her daily vigil by the river, praying for his safety. Her devotion never wavered, even as loneliness weakened her body.

One fateful night, as rain poured endlessly, Sampaguita whispered her final prayer and closed her eyes, still clinging to the promise that love never breaks.

At dawn, the villagers searched for Sampaguita but could not find her. In her place bloomed small white flowers, filling the air with a sweet fragrance.

The elders said the flowers carried her final words: “Sumpa kita” —a sacred promise.

From that day on, the Sampaguita flower became a symbol of purity, loyalty, and love that never fades.
