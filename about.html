Behind the Legend

The legend of the Sampaguita is a well-known Filipino folklore that explains the origin of the country’s national flower. It is believed that the flower was born from a woman’s pure and undying love.

According to the story, her sorrow and devotion were transformed by nature into something beautiful, leaving behind a flower with a gentle fragrance and pure white petals.

In Filipino culture, the Sampaguita represents loyalty, humility, and faithfulness—values that are deeply respected in relationships and family life.

This legend shows how Filipinos place great importance on promises, especially those made out of love and sincerity.

While creating this story, I realized that pain and sacrifice can lead to something meaningful and lasting.

The Sampaguita reminds us that true love and devotion never fade, even with time, distance, or loss.
