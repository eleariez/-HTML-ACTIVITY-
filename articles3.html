Influence of Social Media on Teenagers

The influence of social media on adolescents is significant, as they are both developmentally vulnerable and among the heaviest users of online platforms. According to Alderbrook School, 75% of teens in America have social media profiles, with Facebook being the main platform for 68% of them. Teens use social media for communication, entertainment, and identity formation.

Social feedback through likes, comments, and followers can impact self-esteem and social confidence. Understanding the benefits and risks, and guiding teens to use social media mindfully, is essential for their emotional and social development.

Source: Alderbrook School Report (2021)

The Use of Social Media Modifies Teenagers' Sleep

Access to social media in teenagers’ bedrooms has been linked to reduced sleep time during school weeks, negatively affecting daily functioning and mood. According to research from Europe PMC, these effects increase with age, and early education about sleep and social media usage is essential.

Parents can improve sleep quality by setting controlled bedtimes, limiting screen exposure in the evening, and teaching teens about the impact of blue light from screens.

Source: Europe PMC (2017)

Social Media, Teenagers, and the School Context

This scoping review Springer examined 10 years of research on social media in education. It found that social media is widely studied in teaching, learning, digital literacy, and identity formation. Research also highlighted the importance of addressing negative behaviors, health issues, and social relationships outside education.

Teachers and researchers are encouraged to incorporate lessons from cross-disciplinary research, ensuring a balanced understanding of social media’s role in teens’ educational and personal lives.

Source: Springer Research (2020)

Teenagers, Screens, and Social Media

A narrative review of key studies Springer shows that adolescent responses to digital technologies vary widely. Researchers call for improved transparency, better measurement of effect sizes, and consideration of individual differences that shape how teens experience social media.

Each teen interacts uniquely with online platforms, and effective guidance requires understanding their specific patterns, motivations, and emotional responses.

Source: Springer Narrative Review (2019)

Exploring the Potential Benefits of Using Social Media in Education

Social media offers multiple advantages in educational contexts. According to research ResearchGate, online social tools can improve collaboration, engagement, and resource sharing among students and teachers.

Responsible use can foster creativity, problem-solving, teamwork, and digital literacy, preparing students for modern learning environments and future careers.

Source: ResearchGate (2015)

Educating Teens about Risks on Social Networking Sites

Educational interventions in secondary schools Comunicar show that raising awareness of social media risks can change teen attitudes and behaviors. Teaching responsible usage, privacy awareness, and digital safety is critical for healthy online engagement.

While educational packages are effective, ongoing research is needed to identify the best strategies for behavior change and to ensure that teens act safely and responsibly online.

Source: Comunicar Study (2020)
